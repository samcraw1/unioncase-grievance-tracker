## Introduction

The USPS Grievance Tracker is a comprehensive web application designed to help USPS employees, union stewards, and representatives manage and track grievances through the entire resolution process. This system streamlines the grievance filing process, improves communication, and ensures deadlines are met.

### Key Features

- File and track grievances online
- Upload supporting documents
- Monitor progress through grievance steps
- Track important deadlines
- Add notes and communicate with stewards
- Receive email notifications for updates
- Export grievance details to PDF
- Access from desktop or mobile device
- Install as a Progressive Web App (PWA)

## Getting Started

### Creating an Account

1. **Navigate to the registration page**
   - Visit the application URL
   - Click "Register" or "Create Account"

2. **Fill in your information**
   - Email address (required)
   - Password (minimum 6 characters)
   - First and Last Name
   - Employee ID (optional but recommended)
   - Select your role (Employee, Steward, or Representative)
   - Select your craft (City Carrier, Clerk, Rural Carrier, etc.)
   - Facility name
   - Phone number (optional)

3. **Submit the form**
   - Click "Register"
   - You'll be automatically logged in
   - Your union type will be auto-detected based on your craft

### Logging In

1. **Go to the login page**
   - Enter your email address
   - Enter your password
   - Click "Login"

2. **Stay logged in**
   - Your session will remain active for 7 days
   - You can log out anytime from the Settings page

### First Time Setup

After logging in for the first time:

1. **Review your profile**
   - Click your name in the top right
   - Verify your information is correct

2. **Set notification preferences** (Settings page)
   - Choose which email notifications you want to receive
   - Set deadline reminder preferences (3 days, 1 day, day of)

3. **Explore the dashboard**
   - View the welcome screen
   - Familiarize yourself with the navigation

## For Employees

### Filing a New Grievance

1. **Navigate to "New Grievance"**
   - Click the "+ New Grievance" button in the navigation

2. **Fill in the Incident Information**
   - **Grievant Name**: Your full name or the affected employee
   - **Employee ID**: Your USPS employee ID
   - **Facility**: Your work location
   - **Craft**: Your job classification
   - **Incident Date**: When the violation occurred
   - **Incident Time**: (Optional) Specific time of incident

3. **Contract Information**
   - **Contract Article**: The specific article violated (e.g., "Article 8.5.D")
   - **Violation Type**: Select from dropdown or enter custom
   - **Brief Description**: One-sentence summary (50-100 characters)
   - **Detailed Description**: Full explanation of what happened

4. **Additional Information**
   - **Management Representative**: Name and title of supervisor involved
   - **Witnesses**: List any witnesses (one per line)
   - **Steward Assignment**: (Optional) Select a specific steward

5. **Submit the Grievance**
   - Review all information for accuracy
   - Click "Submit Grievance"
   - You'll receive a confirmation with a grievance number (e.g., GRVNC-2024-0001)

### Viewing Your Grievances

1. **Dashboard Overview**
   - See a summary of all your grievances
   - View active, resolved, and pending counts
   - See upcoming deadlines

2. **Grievance List**
   - Click on any grievance to view details
   - Filter by status or step
   - Search by grievance number

3. **Grievance Details**
   - View all information about the grievance
   - See the timeline of actions taken
   - Check upcoming deadlines
   - Read notes from stewards
   - View uploaded documents

### Adding Documents

1. **Go to the grievance details page**
2. **Click "Upload Document"**
3. **Select a file**
   - Supported formats: PDF, JPEG, PNG, DOC, DOCX, TXT
   - Maximum size: 10MB
4. **Add a label** (e.g., "Time card", "Email evidence")
5. **Add description** (optional)
6. **Click "Upload"**

**Tips for Documents:**
- Upload photos of time cards, schedules, or relevant emails
- Clearly label each document
- Upload documents as soon as possible
- Keep file sizes reasonable (compress large photos)

### Adding Notes

1. **Navigate to the grievance details page**
2. **Scroll to the Notes section**
3. **Type your note in the text box**
4. **Click "Add Note"**
5. **Your note will appear with a timestamp**

**Best Practices for Notes:**
- Document all conversations with management
- Record dates and times of follow-up actions
- Keep notes factual and professional
- Add updates after any developments

### Tracking Progress

**Grievance Steps:**
1. **Filed**: Initial submission
2. **Informal Step A**: Discussion with supervisor
3. **Formal Step A**: Written grievance with formal response
4. **Step B**: Appeal to district level
5. **Arbitration**: Final binding arbitration
6. **Resolved/Settled/Denied**: Final outcomes

**How to Track:**
- Check the "Current Step" indicator on each grievance
- View the timeline to see when each step was completed
- Monitor deadlines to ensure timely progression
- Set up email notifications for status changes

### Exporting to PDF

1. **Open the grievance you want to export**
2. **Click "Export to PDF"** button (top right)
3. **The PDF will download** with all grievance details
4. **Save for your records**

The PDF includes:
- Complete grievance information
- Timeline of all actions
- All notes (non-internal)
- Deadlines and their status

## For Stewards

As a steward, you have additional responsibilities and capabilities.

### Managing Assigned Grievances

1. **View All Assigned Grievances**
   - Your dashboard shows grievances assigned to you
   - You'll also see your own grievances
   - Filter to see only assigned cases

2. **Accept Assignment**
   - Review new grievances assigned to you
   - Add an initial note acknowledging receipt
   - Contact the grievant to discuss the case

### Updating Grievance Steps

1. **Navigate to the grievance**
2. **Click "Update Step"**
3. **Select the new step** from dropdown
4. **Add notes** explaining the update
5. **Click "Save"**

**Important:**
- Always add detailed notes when updating steps
- Document meeting dates and outcomes
- Note any management responses or offers

### Adding Internal Notes

Internal notes are visible only to stewards and representatives, not to employees.

1. **Go to the grievance details page**
2. **In the notes section, check "Internal Note"**
3. **Write your note**
4. **Click "Add Note"**

**Use internal notes for:**
- Strategy discussions
- Confidential information
- Assessment of case strength
- Next steps planning

### Communicating with Employees

**Best Practices:**
- Respond to grievances within 24 hours
- Keep employees informed of progress
- Use notes for documentation
- Consider phone calls for complex issues
- Document all phone conversations in notes

### Meeting Deadlines

1. **Check the Deadlines tab** on each grievance
2. **Set reminders** using the notification settings
3. **Take action before deadlines**
   - Informal Step A: 14 days from incident
   - Formal Step A: Within timeframes specified in contract
   - Step B: As specified in management's response

4. **Mark deadlines complete** when actions are taken

### Resources Page

Access union resources:
- Contract reference materials
- Article explanations
- Past arbitration decisions
- Forms and templates
- Contact information for representatives

## Common Tasks

### Changing Your Password

1. Go to **Settings**
2. Click **"Change Password"**
3. Enter current password
4. Enter new password (minimum 6 characters)
5. Confirm new password
6. Click **"Save"**

### Updating Notification Preferences

1. Navigate to **Settings**
2. Go to **"Notifications"** section
3. Toggle email notifications on/off
4. Select which types of notifications to receive:
   - New grievances (stewards only)
   - Deadline reminders
   - Status updates
   - New notes
   - Resolution notifications
5. Set reminder days (e.g., 7, 3, 1 days before deadline)
6. Click **"Save Preferences"**

### Searching for Grievances

1. **Use the search bar** at the top of the grievance list
2. **Search by:**
   - Grievance number
   - Grievant name
   - Contract article
   - Facility
3. **Use filters** to narrow results:
   - Status (active, resolved, etc.)
   - Current step
   - Date range

### Understanding Deadline Colors

The system uses color coding for deadlines:
- **Green**: More than 7 days remaining
- **Yellow**: 3-7 days remaining
- **Orange**: 1-2 days remaining
- **Red**: Due today or overdue

## Troubleshooting

### Can't Log In

**Forgot Password:**
- Click "Forgot Password" on the login page
- Enter your email address
- Check your email for reset instructions

**Account Issues:**
- Verify you're using the correct email address
- Check that Caps Lock is off
- Clear browser cache and try again
- Contact your steward or system administrator

### Grievance Not Appearing

**If a newly filed grievance doesn't appear:**
1. Refresh the page (pull down on mobile)
2. Check your internet connection
3. Verify the grievance was submitted (check for confirmation message)
4. Try logging out and back in

### Document Upload Fails

**Common issues:**
- File too large (max 10MB)
  - Solution: Compress the file or take a lower resolution photo
- Unsupported file type
  - Solution: Convert to PDF, JPEG, or PNG
- Poor internet connection
  - Solution: Wait for better connection or try on WiFi

### Email Notifications Not Received

1. Check your spam/junk folder
2. Verify notification preferences in Settings
3. Ensure your email address is correct in your profile
4. Check with your email provider (some block automated emails)

### Page Loading Slowly

1. Check your internet connection
2. Try closing other browser tabs
3. Clear browser cache
4. Try a different browser
5. Contact your IT department if on work network

### Mobile App Issues

**If the PWA isn't working:**
1. Make sure you've installed it correctly
2. Try removing and re-adding to home screen
3. Update your phone's browser
4. Try accessing via mobile browser first

## FAQ

### General Questions

**Q: Who can file a grievance?**
A: Any USPS employee covered by the union contract can file a grievance.

**Q: How long does the grievance process take?**
A: It varies by case, but typically:
- Informal Step A: 14 days
- Formal Step A: 10-14 days for response
- Step B: 30-60 days
- Arbitration: Several months

**Q: Can I file a grievance anonymously?**
A: No, grievances must include the grievant's name. However, you can discuss concerns with your steward confidentially before filing.

**Q: Can I withdraw a grievance?**
A: Yes, contact your steward to discuss withdrawing a grievance. This should be documented with notes.

**Q: What if I miss a deadline?**
A: Contact your steward immediately. Some deadlines can be extended by agreement, but it's critical to meet all timelines.

### Technical Questions

**Q: Is my data secure?**
A: Yes, the application uses industry-standard encryption and security practices. All connections are secured with HTTPS, and passwords are encrypted.

**Q: Can I access the system from home?**
A: Yes, the application is web-based and can be accessed from any device with internet connection.

**Q: What browsers are supported?**
A: Modern versions of Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge are fully supported.

**Q: Is there a mobile app?**
A: Yes, the application can be installed as a Progressive Web App (PWA) on both iOS and Android devices.

**Q: Can I access old grievances?**
A: Yes, all grievances remain in the system and can be accessed at any time.

### Process Questions

**Q: What should I include in my detailed description?**
A: Include:
- What happened (facts)
- When it happened (date/time)
- Who was involved
- What contract article was violated
- How you were harmed
- What remedy you're seeking

**Q: How many documents can I upload?**
A: There's no limit on the number of documents, but each must be under 10MB.

**Q: What if management denies my grievance?**
A: Your steward will discuss options for appealing to the next step (Step B or arbitration).

**Q: Can I have multiple active grievances?**
A: Yes, you can file and track multiple grievances simultaneously.

**Q: What happens if I switch facilities?**
A: Your grievances follow you in the system. Update your facility in your profile settings.

### For Stewards

**Q: How do I know when a new grievance is assigned to me?**
A: You'll receive an email notification (if enabled) and see it on your dashboard.

**Q: Can I assign a grievance to another steward?**
A: Contact your representative to have grievances reassigned.

**Q: What if I need help with a case?**
A: Use internal notes to ask questions to representatives, or call them directly for urgent matters.

**Q: How do I track all my assigned cases?**
A: Use the filter on your dashboard to view only cases assigned to you.

## Best Practices

### For Filing Grievances

1. **Act quickly** - File as soon as possible after the incident
2. **Be specific** - Include dates, times, and names
3. **Be factual** - Stick to facts, not opinions
4. **Include witnesses** - List anyone who saw the incident
5. **Upload evidence** - Add documents immediately
6. **Follow up** - Check status regularly

### For Documentation

1. **Keep contemporaneous notes** - Document as events happen
2. **Be professional** - Use appropriate language
3. **Be thorough** - More detail is better than less
4. **Save everything** - Keep copies of all correspondence
5. **Take photos** - Visual evidence is powerful

### For Communication

1. **Respond promptly** - Reply to steward inquiries within 24 hours
2. **Be clear** - Communicate simply and directly
3. **Be professional** - Maintain a respectful tone
4. **Document conversations** - Add notes after phone calls
5. **Use the system** - Keep all communication in the app when possible

## Glossary

- **Grievance**: A formal complaint about a contract violation
- **Grievant**: The person filing the grievance
- **Step**: A level in the grievance resolution process
- **Steward**: Union representative who handles grievances
- **Representative**: Senior union official
- **Article**: Section of the union contract
- **Settlement**: Agreement reached to resolve grievance
- **Arbitration**: Final binding decision by neutral third party
- **Timeline**: Chronological record of grievance actions
- **Deadline**: Date by which an action must be completed
- **Internal Note**: Note visible only to stewards/representatives
